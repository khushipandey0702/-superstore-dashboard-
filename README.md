# Super Store Sales Dashboard  Power BI

Hey! This is one of my first Power BI projects and I am really happy with how it turned out. I built this dashboard using the Sample Superstore dataset which is a popular practice dataset used by a lot of data enthusiasts.

The idea was simple  take raw sales data and turn it into something that actually makes sense visually and helps answer real business questions.



## Why I Built This

I have been learning Power BI for a while now and I wanted to work on something that felt like a real project rather than just following tutorials. So I picked this dataset, explored it myself and tried to build a dashboard that tells a proper story.

---

## What the Dashboard Covers

- **Sales vs Last Year** — How much did we sell this year compared to last year
- **Profit Analysis** — Which products and categories are actually making money
- **Product Performance** — Breakdown of profit across Furniture, Office Supplies and Technology
- **Regional Performance** — Which states in the US are doing well and which ones are not
- **Customer Segments** — Who is buying the most — Consumers, Corporate or Home Office
- **Return Rate** — How many orders are being returned and is it improving
- **Monthly Trends** — How sales have moved month by month over the years



## Interesting Things I Found in the Data

- Technology is the most profitable category even though Furniture has decent sales
- Tables in the Furniture category are actually losing money — this surprised me
- California and New York are by far the strongest states
- The Consumer segment makes up about 50% of all sales
- Return rate has improved from 8.74% to 5.79% which is a good sign


## What I Learned

Honestly this project taught me a lot more than just Power BI. A few things I picked up:

- How to clean and shape data before building visuals
- Writing basic DAX formulas for calculated measures
- Thinking about layout and design  not just dumping charts on a page
- Picking the right chart for the right data
- How to make a dashboard that actually tells a story instead of just showing numbers
